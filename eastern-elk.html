Eastern Elk

The Eastern Elk (*Cervus canadensis canadensis*) was an extinct subspecies of elk that inhabited the Northern and eastern united states, and southern canada. The last eastern elk was shot dead on september 1,1877 in Pennsylvania.

Eastern Elks inhabited areas from the Appalachian Mountains to the Great Lakes region. They thrived in forested landscapes, grazing on grasses, shrubs, and tree bark. However, their large size and plentiful numbers made them an easy target for overhunting.

By the late 19th century, habitat loss due to deforestation and unregulated hunting led to their extinction. The last confirmed sighting of the Eastern Elk was in Pennsylvania in the 1870s. Today, efforts to reintroduce closely related subspecies, such as the Rocky Mountain Elk, aim to restore some of their ecological role.
