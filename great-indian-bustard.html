Great Indian Bustard

The Great Indian Bustard (*Ardeotis nigriceps*) is one of the largest flying birds in India and was once found in large numbers across the Indian subcontinent. With its majestic stature, the bird became a symbol of India's rich biodiversity.

This bird prefers arid grasslands and scrublands, where it feeds on insects, small mammals, and seeds. Its distinctive long legs and neck make it easily identifiable, and it was once widespread in regions such as Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Maharashtra.

However, the Great Indian Bustard has suffered a dramatic decline in its population due to habitat loss, hunting, and disturbance from human activities like farming and infrastructure development. The bird's large size and slow movements made it vulnerable to poaching, further exacerbating its decline.

Today, the Great Indian Bustard is critically endangered, with only a few hundred individuals remaining in the wild. Conservation efforts are underway to protect its remaining habitats, reduce human-wildlife conflict, and prevent illegal hunting. However, much work remains to ensure the survival of this iconic species.
