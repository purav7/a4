Blue Walleye

The Blue Walleye (*Sander vitreus glaucus*), a subspecies of the walleye, was native to the Great Lakes in North America, particularly Lake Erie and Lake Ontario. It was distinguished by its bluish sheen, which gave it its name, and its importance as a food source for local communities and wildlife.

Due to overfishing and habitat degradation, the Blue Walleye's population began to decline rapidly in the mid-20th century. Increased pollution in the Great Lakes and competition from other fish species further contributed to its extinction.

The Blue Walleye was officially declared extinct in 1983, making it a somber example of how human activity can impact aquatic ecosystems. Today, efforts to preserve other native species in the Great Lakes emphasize habitat restoration and sustainable fishing practices.
