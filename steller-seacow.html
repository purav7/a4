Steller's Sea Cow

Steller's Sea Cow (*Hydrodamalis gigas*) was a massive marine mammal that belonged to the order Sirenia, which includes manatees and dugongs. This species was first documented in 1741 by German naturalist Georg Wilhelm Steller during an expedition near the Commander Islands in the Bering Sea.

Weighing up to 10 tons and measuring about 9 meters (30 feet) in length, Steller's Sea Cow lived in the cold waters of the North Pacific. It fed on kelp and other marine vegetation, and its slow movements made it an easy target for hunters.

Within 27 years of its discovery, Steller's Sea Cow was hunted to extinction by fur traders and sailors who exploited its meat, hide, and fat. The last known individual was killed in 1768, marking a tragic end for this unique species.

The loss of Steller's Sea Cow serves as a stark reminder of the devastating impact of overhunting and the need for sustainable practices to protect marine life.
