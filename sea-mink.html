Sea Mink

The Sea Mink (*Neovison macrodon*) was a species of mink native to the coasts of Maine, USA, and the Canadian Maritime provinces. Larger than its terrestrial relatives, the Sea Mink had a dense, waterproof fur coat that made it highly sought after by fur traders.

This species thrived in coastal environments, feeding on fish, shellfish, and other small marine creatures. Its semi-aquatic lifestyle and reliance on marine habitats made it unique among minks.

Unfortunately, the Sea Mink was relentlessly hunted for its luxurious fur, which was in high demand during the 19th century. Overexploitation and habitat destruction led to its extinction, with the last confirmed sighting reported in the late 1800s.

The tragic story of the Sea Mink underscores the importance of regulating wildlife trade and preserving critical habitats for vulnerable species.
