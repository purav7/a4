Asiatic Lioness

The Asiatic Lioness (*Panthera leo persica*) is a subspecies of lion that is currently found only in the Gir Forest of Gujarat, India. These majestic creatures are slightly smaller than their African counterparts and are characterized by their less developed manes, especially in males, which makes the lionesses more distinguishable.

Historically, Asiatic lions roamed vast territories across the Middle East and parts of Asia, from Turkey to India. However, habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and hunting have drastically reduced their population. By the early 20th century, they were confined to the Gir Forest.

Thanks to conservation efforts, the Asiatic lion population has shown a steady increase in recent decades. As of 2020, their population was estimated at around 674 individuals. While this is a significant improvement, they remain endangered, and continued efforts are essential to ensure their survival in the wild.
