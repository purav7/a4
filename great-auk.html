Great Auk

The Great Auk (*Pinguinus impennis*) was a large, flightless bird native to the North Atlantic. It was once found along the coasts of Canada, Greenland, Iceland, and northern Europe.

Measuring about 75–85 cm (29–33 inches) in height, the Great Auk was an excellent swimmer and diver, feeding primarily on fish. It nested in dense colonies on remote islands, which unfortunately made it an easy target for human hunters.

During the 16th to 19th centuries, the Great Auk was hunted extensively for its meat, eggs, and down feathers. By the early 19th century, the population had dwindled dramatically. The last known pair was killed in 1844 on Eldey Island, near Iceland.

The Great Auk's extinction highlights the consequences of overexploitation and serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of wildlife conservation.
